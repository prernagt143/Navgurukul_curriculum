        Simple Introduction to Flow Charts:

        A flowchart is like a map that shows the steps of a process or a sequence of actions. It uses different shapes and arrows to illustrate each step and how they connect to each other.

        Imagine you're baking a cake â€“ you'd start with gathering ingredients, then mix them, bake the batter, and finally, enjoy your cake!

          Similarly, in a flowchart:
        • Start: You begin at a starting point, often represented by a rounded shape or an oval.
        • Actions: Each action or step in the process is represented by a rectangle. For example, "Mix Ingredients" or "Bake Cake."
        • Decisions: Sometimes, you have to make decisions during a process, like "Is the cake done baking?" These are represented by diamonds. Depending on the answer, the flowchart might follow different paths.
        • Arrows: Arrows show the flow of the process, connecting each step from start to finish. They show the direction the process moves in.
        • End: Finally, there's an endpoint, usually represented by a rounded shape or oval, indicating that the process has been completed.

          • Flowcharts are used in many fields, like programming, business, and engineering, to map out processes, troubleshoot problems, and plan projects. They're a great way to visualize complex processes and understand them better.
